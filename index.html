2026 Projections

This Year's Outlook

After several years of historically early blooms, 2026 is shaping up differently. The colder-than-normal winter has kept the trees firmly dormant, and the National Park Service is projecting peak bloom will arrive later than we've seen in recent years, likely late March into early April.

🌸 2026 Peak Bloom Projection

Later Than Recent Years

Due to this winter's record cold, the Yoshino cherry trees are still fully dormant as of late February. NPS spokesman Mike Litterst confirmed bloom will come "later in the season than we've seen for the last few years." No official forecast has been issued yet, NPS considers predictions reliable only within ~10 days but the festival window provides the target range.

March 28th Earliest Realistic Peak
April 1st-10th Most Likely Window
Mar 20 – Apr 12 Festival Dates (Official)

NPS official forecast releases ~March 1. Monitor ebird.org/alert and the NPS BloomCam for live updates.

Peak bloom refers to 70% of Yoshino blossoms being open. Once declared, the window typically lasts 3–7 days, weather permitting. A warm spell, rain, or wind can strip petals overnight or rush the cycle. The days before peak are often just as stunning, with fewer crowds.

Where to Go

Premier Blossom Locations

DC's cherry trees grow in three primary NPS locations, plus several lesser-known spots. Each spot has its own character and crowd profile.

01
Tidal Basin
West Potomac Park · The Iconic Loop
The crown jewel and typical postcard shot: Washington Monument and Jefferson Memorial framed in pink. The 1.75-mile loop path encircles the basin and is lined with hundreds of Yoshino cherry trees. Peak bloom here is what the NPS officially declares. Expect massive crowds 8am–5pm on any weekend during peak. Tactic: arrive before sunrise. The BloomCam streams live at the NPS website.
Yoshino Sunrise Magic Seawall Construction 2026
02
East Potomac Park / Hains Point
SW Washington DC · 1,100+ Trees
The local secret for photographers. Over 1,100 cherry trees on a peninsula jutting into the Potomac with fewer crowds than the Tidal Basin, and the Kwanzan variety here blooms 1–2 weeks after the Yoshinos, extending your viewing window significantly. The golf course fairways fill with fallen petals like pink snow. Also DC's prime rare bird and gull hotspot in winter.
Kwanzan Lower Crowds Later Bloom
03
Washington Monument Grounds
National Mall · Open Sight Lines
Cherry trees ring the base of the Monument, providing unobstructed 360° framing shots. Tends to be slightly less crowded than the Tidal Basin loop while still delivering the iconic monumental backdrop. The wide open turf also makes a good spot to lay on the ground and shoot upward through the branches.
Monument Views Low Crowds
04
Kenilworth Park & Aquatic Gardens
NE Washington DC · Near Anacostia Station
Cherry blossoms here aren't the main event, but the surrounding seasonal blooms and tidal marsh create a layered spring scene that serious naturalists love. By April the saucer magnolias and early warblers arrive simultaneously. Then in July, the lotus blooms transform this place into something otherworldly.
Multi-Season Magnolias July Lotus
05
Enid A. Haupt Garden (Smithsonian)
National Mall · South of Castle
The saucer magnolias here bloom roughly 1–2 weeks before the Yoshino cherries as a stunning preview show and substantially more impressive than most people expect. When the cherry trees also come into bloom, this garden is one of the quietest spots on the Mall. Free, always open, almost never crowded.
Saucer Magnolias First Low Crowds
06
Rawlins Park
Dupont Circle Area · Neighborhood Gem
A small urban park near Dupont Circle that erupts with saucer magnolias and early flowering cherries before the Tidal Basin peaks. A favorite among locals and walkable from the Red Line. Great for pre-peak reconnaissance without all of the tourists.
Local Secret Metro Access Early Preview
07
Capitol Grounds
Capitol Hill · East & West Fronts
Cherry trees line the paths around the Capitol's East and West Fronts. The East Front plaza has a cluster of Yoshinos that bloom alongside the iconic dome backdrop — an underused vantage with easy Metro access from Capitol South. Crowds are a fraction of the Tidal Basin, and the architectural framing is unmatched.
Monument Views Metro Access Low Crowds
08
Dumbarton Oaks
Georgetown · R St NW
A 27-acre historic garden in Georgetown with a Japanese-influenced terrace and cherry trees that bloom slightly ahead of the Tidal Basin due to the sheltered microclimate. Small admission fee, but delivers near-zero crowds and impeccably maintained grounds. The formal garden design makes for exceptional photography even on cloudy days.
Early Bloom Georgetown Admission Required
09
US National Arboretum
NE Washington DC · New York Ave NE
446 acres of ornamental trees and shrubs including multiple cherry varieties across sweeping hillsides. The Azalee Collection and National Bonsai pavilion are peak-season bonuses. This place is essentially unknown to casual visitors — free parking, tranquil trails, and a level of variety you won't find anywhere else in the city. The Glenn Dale Azaleas typically peak alongside or just after the cherries.
Multiple Varieties Free Parking Low Crowds

Records to know: Earliest ever: March 15, 1990 (exceptionally warm winter). Latest ever: April 18, 1958 (exceptionally cold, similar to this year's pattern). Long-run average since 1921: April 3. The bloom has arrived before April 4 in 16 of the past 20 years. The 2024 bloom (March 17) was the second-earliest on record, driven by an unusually warm February and March.

Phenology

The Six Stages of Bloom

Understanding where the trees are in their cycle is how you know when to visit them! NPS tracks six defined stages, with stage 6 being the moment everyone's chasing.

01 🌿 Green Buds Dormancy breaks and the first green tips become visible on branch tips. Usually late Feb–early March.
02 🔍 Florets Visible Green bud opens slightly and individual flower clusters (florets) become distinguishable.
03 📏 Extension of Florets Florets elongate and separate, and pale color begins to show through the green.
04 🌱 Peduncle Elongation The flower stem (peduncle) stretches and blossoms start to hang and become clearly flower-shaped.
05 🤍 Puffy White Blossoms are nearly open but still closed, appearing puffy and white. This is 1–3 days from peak, visit now to beat crowds.
06 🌸 Peak Bloom 70%+ of Yoshino blossoms open = the declared peak. Lasts 3–7 days.

Tip: Stage 5 ("puffy white") is an underrated time to visit. The trees look spectacular, and crowds are noticeably thinner than on the declared peak bloom date. In 2024, green buds appeared March 2 and peak bloom arrived just 15 days later (an unusually compressed cycle). In 2008, the same process took five weeks.

History & Science

Things Worth Knowing

🇯🇵
The 1912 Gift
Tokyo Mayor Yukio Ozaki gifted 3,020 cherry trees to Washington in 1912 as a symbol of friendship between Japan and the United States. The original shipment of 2,000 trees in 1910 was destroyed as they arrived diseased and had to be burned. The replacement gift in 1912 was the successful planting. Today the stock has grown to approximately 3,800 trees across multiple varieties.
🌡️
The Climate Signal
Peak bloom has trended ~1 week earlier over the past century. From 1931–1960, average peak was April 6. From 1981–2010, it was April 1. The 2015–2024 average is March 27. Climate Central projects bloom as early as early March by 2070. The 2024 bloom (March 17) was the second-earliest on record. 2026's cold winter is going to be an outlier.
🌸
11 Varieties, One Famous One
There are 11 varieties of cherry blossom in DC. The most famous, and the ones that create the iconic white cloud effect at the Tidal Basin, are Yoshino (Prunus × yedoensis). The Kwanzan variety at East Potomac Park blooms 1–2 weeks later with deeper pink, double-petaled flowers. They give you a second act if you miss the Yoshino window.
❄️
The Frost Danger Window
Once blossoms open, they're vulnerable. The NPS reports damage begins at 27°F and becomes significant below 24°F. A late frost during Stage 5 or 6 can brown and collapse the entire bloom overnight. Cold is not the trees' enemy during dormancy, only during the open bloom window. The 2026 cold winter actually builds resilience, but danger arrives once they open.
🏗️
The Tidal Basin Construction
Phase 1 of a major seawall reconstruction is complete, addressing decades of sea level rise and flooding that threatened the root systems of the cherry trees. Some trees were removed for the project in 2024. New saplings are being added in 2026. The southern corner of the basin will still show construction in spring 2026, but most of the loop remains accessible.
📸
The Crowd Math
The cherry blossoms draw over 1.5 million visitors annually with the majority concentrated into the peak bloom window of perhaps 5–7 days. The Tidal Basin loop is only 1.75 miles. That math is brutal on weekends between 9am–6pm. The move: arrive 30 min before sunrise and leave before 9am, visit mid-week, or wait for Stage 5 the weekend prior to peak.

Tracking & Monitoring

Build a system where the trees come to you! All of this is free and takes 10 minutes to set up.

Live Monitoring
NPS Bloom Watch + BloomCam
The National Park Service runs the official bloom stage tracker and a live BloomCam pointed at the Tidal Basin. This is the authoritative source. Stage updates post when conditions change, typically every few days leading up to peak.
nps.gov/cherryblossom → bloomcam.org →
Independent Tracking
Cherry Blossom Watch
Independently run photo-based tracker updated frequently with on-the-ground observations. Aggregates all major forecasts (NPS, Washington Post, Storm Team 4) in one place. Often updated more frequently than the NPS page during the approach to peak.
cherryblossomwatch.com → Subscribe to email updates →
Flock Migration Intelligence
BirdCast
Uses NEXRAD radar to track actual migration in real time. Spring cherry blossom visits coincide with the beginning of warbler migration and BirdCast tells you when a wave is coming. Plan your Tidal Basin visit for a morning after a south wind and you're likely to log Ospreys, early warblers, Great Blue Herons, and migrant sparrows simultaneously.
birdcast.info →

National Cherry Blossom Festival
The 2026 festival runs March 20 – April 12. Events include the Sakura Matsuri Japanese Street Festival, the Kite Festival, Petalpalooza, and the parade. Many events are free. The Cherry Blossom 10 Mile Run is April 12. Schedule and tickets at the official site.
